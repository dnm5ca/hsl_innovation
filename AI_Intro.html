Intro to Artificial Intelligence

With the rising popularity of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Large Language Models (LLMs), understanding these technologies is more important than ever. This webpage will guide you through the basics of AI, including its definition, ethical considerations, and practical guidelines for its use.

What is artificial Intelligence (AI)

Artificial intelligence (AI) encompasses a broad field that is primarily interested in training intelligent machines which can help solve problems. From that broad categorization, AI can be broken down into more specific layers:

  • Artificial intelligence: The broadest category, encompassing all areas involved in creating intelligent machines.
  • Machine learning: A large category that focuses on pattern recognition in structured data, often stored in tabular format, and frequently employs statistical methods. For instance, linear regression is a supervised machine learning technique.
  • Deep learning: A subset of machine learning that uses artificial neural networks, a computation method inspired by the human brain. Due to its complexity, deep learning excels at handling unstructured data types such as images, text, or audio.
  • Large language models: Specifically designed for text, AI chatbots like ChatGPT are based on LLMs trained on particular data sets. LLMs are also a form of generative AI, as they can produce new content.

    • General Guidelines for AI Use

    If you are new to working with AI, there are a few key principles to keep in mind. The UVA Arts & Sciences Learning Design and Technology group has provided a few foundational ideas on how to utilize generative AI (GenAI) that are also provided below:

    Foundational Ideas

    1. Human Verification: There must always be a "human in the loop" to verify the output from AI.
    2. Dynamic Responses: Generative AI is not a web search; it will generate a new response each time.
    3. Conversational tone: You are conversing with AI, not just asking it questions. The way you write your prompt is important.
    4. Shared Exploration: We are all engaged in the collective exploration and development of AI.

    It is crucial to remember that the primary goal of LLMs is to generate human-like text, not necessarily true statements. A flow chart from a UNESCO report suggests that the first question to ask when using ChatGPT or a similar AI is, "Does it matter if the output is true?" When utilizing generative AI, it is essential to have the expertise to validate the output and to accept responsibility for any decisions or final products.

    Data Privacy and Ethical Concerns

    In addition to being cautious about the results produced by generative AI, it is important to be mindful of the information you upload. Depending on the specific model you are using, it may store information from your sessions and use it for training purposes. Even if the company does not use that data for training, the storage of previous sessions for future reference still presents a potential risk of a data breach. The current recommendation from UVA is to not enter sensitive, personal, or proprietary information into any Gen-AI system and to use UVA-licensed AI tools whenever possible.

    Prompting GenAI

    There are many different prompting frameworks, but there are a few key components that are normally included.

    • Instruction: A specific task for the model to perform
    • Context: Additional details or information you can provide the model for better responses
      • Role: Defining a role or persona the AI should model its response after (medical librarian, statistician, manuscript reviewer)
    • Input: Any input data (text or data to be analyzed)
    • Output format: How the output should be formatted (formal text, bullet points, tables, etc. )

    Tips and Tricks

    To effectively use generative AI like ChatGPT, it's helpful to follow certain best practices. These tips can enhance your interaction with the model and improve the quality of the output:

    • Use delimiters (like the triple quotes above) to indicate distinct parts of the input
      • In ChatGPT, use Shift+Enter to create a new line without sending the message
    • Specify the steps required to complete a task
    • Provide examples for the AI to model its response after
    • Give the model time to “think”
      • “Let’s work this out in a step by step way to be sure we have the right answer”
    • Remember this is an iterative process: use follow-up prompts
    • Use LLMs to help you study
      • Generate flash cards
      • Ask a LLM to generate case studies and then practice diagnosing them
      • Generate quizzes and tests
